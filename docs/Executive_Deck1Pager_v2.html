01 — The Challenge
Problem Statement
Operations teams manage a complex ecosystem of systems with no unified automated monitoring — leading to manual effort, delayed response, and SLA risk.
  • Fragmented Monitoring — GCP, K8s, Sailpoint, Forgerock, PGSQL, and ServiceNow monitored separately with no single pane of glass
  • Manual Incident Handling — Incidents sit in “New” until manually acknowledged, adding 30-40% to resolution time
  • No SLA Reports — Uptime and Incident SLA reports require days of manual data gathering and formatting
  • Inaccurate SLA Calculations — No business-hour logic and no planned downtime exclusion, resulting in unfair metrics
  • Reactive Outage Detection — Application outages discovered only after user complaints; no automated polling or trend tracking
  • Significant FTE Overhead — Manual monitoring, reporting, and cross-system checks consume hundreds of hours annually
02 — What We Built
PulseOps V3
A unified, modular operations intelligence platform that replaces fragmented manual processes with real-time dashboards, automated workflows, and enterprise-grade reporting.
  • Unified Dashboard — Single pane of glass for all systems with real-time SSE updates and live K8s monitoring
  • Auto-Acknowledge Engine — Background poller auto-acks incidents in under 1 minute with full audit trail
  • 1-Click SLA Reports — Automated Uptime, Resolution, and Response SLA reports with PDF export for client delivery
  • Business-Hour SLA Engine — Smart calculations with per-priority thresholds and planned downtime exclusion via ServiceNow
  • 60-Second Health Polling — Proactive minute-by-minute monitoring with status validation, alerting, and historical tracking
  • Zero-Downtime Modules — Hot-drop plug-and-play architecture; build, drop, enable — no restarts needed
